Governing Document

Hall Constitution

The official constitution of Sir Frank Worrell Hall, as amended in November 2022. All election procedures are governed by this document.

The Hall Constitution is the foundational document that governs all aspects of student council operations, elections, and resident rights at Sir Frank Worrell Hall. Every resident is encouraged to read the sections relevant to the election process.

⚡ Sections highlighted in the table of contents are especially relevant to the election process.

Section Summaries

These summaries highlight the key sections of the constitution. Refer to the full document above for authoritative text.

📜
Preamble

States the founding principles of the Hall's student governance structure, including the commitment to democratic representation and the welfare of all residents.

🏛️
Executive Roles

Defines the duties of all nine council positions — from the Deputy Hall Chairperson to the Sports & Games Chairperson — including their term of office and responsibilities.

⚡
Election Procedures

Covers the full election cycle: nomination requirements, campaign conduct rules, voting procedures, result declaration, and dispute resolution mechanisms.

⚡
Eligibility & Voting Rights

Specifies who is eligible to vote, who may stand as a candidate, residency requirements, and the rules governing the Electoral Commission's conduct.

✏️
Amendments

Details the process by which the constitution may be amended, including quorum requirements and the ratification process. This version reflects amendments made in November 2022.
